# Product brief

## Problem

Students often want to learn practical skills outside formal courses, while their peers already hold useful knowledge they would be comfortable sharing. Existing options tend to frame learning as a paid course, professional networking, or an unstructured social interaction. That makes a small, friendly exchange harder to start than it should be.

## Target users

The initial audience is university students who:

- want low-pressure help with a practical, creative, academic, or everyday skill;
- can share something useful without identifying as an expert;
- value meeting peers through a clear shared purpose; and
- need control over how and when an interaction happens.

## Value proposition

Spark helps students discover approachable peers through skills and curiosity, then gives them enough structure to begin learning together safely.

## Product principles

- Everyone has something worth sharing.
- Teaching and learning are equally visible parts of a profile.
- A request should be specific, respectful, and easy to decline.
- People choose their format, availability, and boundaries.
- Safety guidance appears at the moment it is relevant.

## Beta hypothesis

If academically verified students can control profile visibility and use a structured, private request workflow, they will feel comfortable completing peer-learning sessions across devices.

## Initial success metrics

- Percentage of completed profiles that send or receive a request
- Request acceptance rate
- Percentage of accepted requests marked complete
- Median time from discovery to request submission
- Qualitative comfort and trust rating after a session
- Repeat participation as both learner and teacher
